
# Backstory - Glomp
Glomp had previously entered into a commitment with [[The Collector Of Lost Souls]]
It joined their team and was tasked with recruiting more creatures for the cause. What cause? To give the members meaning. To make them feel welcome. And of course, invading The Core in search of the legendary wealth and treasure within. 

However, Glomp was unable to find more members, and was eventually cast out. Clearly the mission statement wasn't completely truthful. This caused Glomp to enter a spiral of feeling unwanted and as though they don't belong anywhere. Eventually they came across the party, and decided that they would make good "ammo" for the Soul Band. Glomp somehow missed the signs, and did not realize the Collector's operation was actually nefarious. They somewhat willingly brought their friends to a not-so-friendly meeting.

The Collector looks up, smiles, and says "Oh dear Glomp, how very nice to see you again..."

# Intro
The AoF dissipates from sight, revealing an opening full of swirling blue energy at the bottom of the pit.

The portal has a strange pulling force. Against the party's will, it drags them into the azure mist within. They enter the portal at the bottom of the [[Fabula Ultima/7-Items/Arcanum of the Forge|Arcanum of the Forge]] pit

They arrive in a land doused in twilight. Sounds of nature echo across the land. Despite the vast soundscape caused by the local flora and fauna; the party simultaneously experiences a deep and almost troubling silence.

Trixie feels a little bit of a shiver, but dismisses it without a second thought.

After the party acclimates a little bit to their new surroundings, they spot a far away figure, dancing off further into the distance.
They attempt to follow the figure, but after a few steps - discover they are trapped in some type of magical dome. The walls of which only become visible as they approached them. As a half-elf, Trixie is familiar with this type of cage. However, as she is lacking most of her memories, most of the details are lost. The one thing she does remember though, is that these were used as a type of intellectual test.
